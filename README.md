# Guns-n-Dragons

Game Title (working title):
Guns n’ Dragons

Elevator Pitch:
A game about a veteran who suddenly gets sent into a strange fantasy world where instead of magic, the creatures in this
world use guns. Hunt and defeat these armed creatures for better weapons, letting you build a stronger and more diverse
arsenal as you progress. As you progress, you adapt to this unusual world while uncovering what’s really going on behind
it.

How does "Growth" connect to your game? (Mechanic, narrative, visual, or all three?)
Growth ties directly into power and mastery. Growth comes from a skill tree and weapon progression. Every creature you
defeat gives you access to better guns, and EXP for upgrades.

Genre / Game Type - Top down and RPG

Player Perspective - 2d Top Down

Core Mechanic: (What does the player DO most of the time?)
The player spends most of the time fighting fantasy creatures in gun-based combat, focusing on defeating enemies to
collect their weapons and upgrade their arsenal. As they progress, players invest in a skill tree that enhances their
abilities and weapons’ stats.

Win / Lose Condition: (How does the game end?)
The game ends with a victory when the player defeats the final boss, a powerful dragon that wields guns. In a lose
condition, the player loses when their health is depleted during combat. Losing resets progress to a checkpoint,
depending on the room.

What excites you most about this idea?
What excites me the most about this idea is the twist. We took the fantasy setting but replaced magic with guns, which immediately feels fresh and unexpected. Our goal is to push the concept to its extreme form with the final boss, being a dragon that wields weapons, instead of the traditional attacks like fireballs.

What's the biggest risk? How will you handle it?
The biggest risk is the potential repetitive gameplay if it means to shoot similar enemies over and over again for better guns and grinding for more EXP. To handle this, the game will require less EXP or skill points to acquire skills from the skill tree. Another risk is balance, since giving players stronger guns too quickly could make the game too easy or remove challenges. This can be managed by pacing upgrades carefully, lowering the ammo count for stronger weapons, and making later enemies require strategy, not just better stats. Keeping the skill tree meaningful, where choices actually change gameplay, will also help maintain long-term engagement.
